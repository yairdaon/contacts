%% \noindent\textbf{Significance Statement.}
%% Spatial epidemic models routinely include connectivity between
%% regions, but we show this parameter is fundamentally unidentifiable
%% for synchronized seasonal diseases. Using the Cram\'er-Rao Lower
%% Bound, we prove that when influenza epidemics rise and fall together
%% across US states each winter, no statistical method can reliably
%% estimate inter-state connectivity from mortality data. This carries a
%% practical message for modelers: if a parameter cannot be learned from
%% data, it should not be in the model. For established seasonal
%% pathogens, simpler models without spatial coupling are equally
%% justified. Connectivity remains identifiable for novel pathogens
%% during their initial asynchronous spread.
