\begin{abstract}
Spatial forecasting models for seasonal-influenza preparedness encode
inter-regional connectivity from external mobility data.
%%
%%~\citep{reich2019, johansson2019}
%%
We ask whether the surveillance data that train such models carry any
information about this parameter. Using the Cram\'er-Rao Lower Bound
on a connectivity parameter, we find that seasonal entrainment causes
inter-regional-coupling to be unidentifiable in epidemic
models. Because the CRLB bounds every unbiased estimator and is
computed from the model alone, this vanishing is established at the
design stage, without fitting messy surveillance data or iterating
through alternative inference pipelines. A validation on 12~seasons of
excess pneumonia and influenza mortality across 47~US states confirms
the prediction: 95\% confidence intervals around estimated
connectivity overlap zero for nearly all 1081~state pairs. The
operational implication is concrete: inter-regional coupling carries
very liitle information and should be dropped from seasonal-influenza
preparedness forecasting models.
\end{abstract}
