\section{Results}

\subsection{Entrainment Inflates the CRLB}

Figure~\ref{fig:crlb_ratio} shows the ratio of median CRLB
(synchronized / asynchronous) across a grid of connectivity values
$\theta$ and seasonal forcing amplitudes $\delta$, with initial
conditions drawn from the empirical distribution of real-data fits
(SI~Fig.~\ref{fig:si_ics}). The ratio exceeds unity throughout the
parameter space, often by orders of magnitude: entrainment inflates
the minimum achievable estimation variance of the connectivity
parameter by factors that can exceed $10^3$ relative to the
asynchronous regime. This confirms, in a simulated setting with
realistic ICs, the entrainment-driven collapse of information about
$\theta$ seen in the real data.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.75\textwidth]{../pix/crlb_ratio.pdf}
\caption{Ratio of median CRLB (synchronized / asynchronous) as a
  function of connectivity $\theta$ and seasonal forcing amplitude
  $\delta$. Initial conditions for each simulated season and region
  are drawn independently from the empirical distribution of
  per-season, per-state values fitted to the real data
  (SI~Fig.~\ref{fig:si_ics}). The ratio exceeds~1 throughout the
  parameter space.}
\label{fig:crlb_ratio}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Data}

We analyze excess P\&I mortality from 47~US states over 12 flu seasons
(2010--2018 and 2023--2025, excluding the COVID-19 pandemic years).
Figure~\ref{fig:seasons} shows the raw weekly death counts for four
representative states, with the flu seasons used for inference
highlighted. The synchronized winter peaks are clearly visible: all
four time series rise and fall together each year.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../pix/seasons_highlighted.pdf}
\caption{Weekly P\&I deaths for four US states. Light traces show the
  full time series; darker segments indicate the 20-week flu seasons
  (starting each November) included in the analysis. COVID-19 pandemic
  years (2020--2022) are excluded. A pre-season baseline is subtracted
  before model fitting.}
\label{fig:seasons}
\end{figure}

\subsection{US Influenza: No Statistical Evidence for Inter-Regional
  Coupling}

We estimated $\theta$ and its CRLB for all $\binom{47}{2} = 1081$
state pairs across 12 seasons. Figure~\ref{fig:theta_vs_crlb}~(left)
shows $\hat\theta$ against $\sqrt{\text{CRLB}}$---the lower bound on
the standard deviation of any unbiased estimator of $\theta$, which
the MLE attains asymptotically. The dashed line marks the 5\%
one-sided significance boundary: points above it have CRLB-derived
95\% confidence intervals that overlap zero. The overwhelming
majority of state pairs sit above the line, meaning that for nearly
every pair of US states the surveillance data provide no statistical
evidence for $\theta > 0$ under any unbiased estimator.

Figure~\ref{fig:theta_vs_crlb}~(right) shows the empirical
distribution of $\hat\theta$ across state pairs. The estimates spread
approximately uniformly over $[0, 0.5]$: because the CRLB is large,
each pair's point estimate is driven by the particular noise
realization in its 12 seasons rather than by a signal in the data
about coupling strength. The location of $\hat\theta$ within
$[0, 0.5]$ is therefore itself statistically uninformative.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../pix/theta_vs_crlb.pdf}
\caption{Left: estimated connectivity $\hat{\theta}$ vs.\
  $\sqrt{\text{CRLB}}$ for all 1081~US state pairs. The dashed line is
  the significance boundary: points above it cannot reject $\theta =
  0$ at the 5\% level. Black circles: non-significant. Red stars:
  significant. Right: distribution of $\hat\theta$ across all pairs,
  showing an approximately uniform spread consistent with
  uninformative data.}
\label{fig:theta_vs_crlb}
\end{figure}
