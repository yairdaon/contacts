\section{Initial-Condition Asymmetry Restores Identifiability}\label{sec:si_ic_asymmetry}

Figure~\ref{fig:si_ic_asymmetry} explores the effect of
initial-condition asymmetry on the likelihood surface under
synchronized (left, $\Delta\varphi = 0$) and asynchronous (right,
$\Delta\varphi = \pi$) forcing. Three IC regimes are compared:
identical ICs for both regions, similar ICs drawn from the empirical
distribution of real-data fits (SI~Fig.~\ref{fig:si_ics}), and very
different ICs drawn from wide ranges.

Under synchronized forcing (left), identical ICs produce a completely
flat likelihood. Similar ICs---representative of the variation
observed between US states---produce only a marginal improvement.
Very different ICs restore a clear peak, demonstrating that
substantial IC asymmetry can compensate for the lack of phase
asymmetry. Under asynchronous forcing (right), all three IC regimes
produce a sharp peak near the true $\theta$, confirming that phase
asymmetry alone is sufficient for identifiability regardless of IC
similarity.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../pix/ic_asymmetry_sync_unsync.pdf}
\caption{Log-likelihood as a function of $\theta$ for three
  initial-condition regimes. Left: synchronized forcing
  ($\Delta\varphi = 0$). Right: asynchronous forcing ($\Delta\varphi
  = \pi$). Red dashed line: true $\theta = 0.05$. Under
  synchronization, only very different ICs restore identifiability.
  Under asynchronous forcing, identifiability is robust to IC
  similarity.}
\label{fig:si_ic_asymmetry}
\end{figure}
