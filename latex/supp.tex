\section{Initial conditions absorb the effect of connectivity}\label{sec:si_schur}
Let
%
\[
  v_\theta := \mathbf{W}^{1/2}\,\frac{\partial\mu}{\partial\theta}
  \in \mathbb{R}^{2T},
  \qquad
  v_\eta := \mathbf{W}^{1/2}\,\frac{\partial\mu}{\partial\eta}
  \in \mathbb{R}^{2T\times 4},
\]
%
where the initial-condition nuisance vector is $\eta = (S_1(0),
I_1(0), S_2(0), I_2(0))$. The Fisher information matrix is
%
\[
  \mathbf{J} = \mathbf{G}^\top\mathbf{W}\mathbf{G}
  = \begin{pmatrix}
    v_\theta^\top v_\theta & v_\theta^\top v_\eta \\[3pt]
    v_\eta^\top v_\theta & v_\eta^\top v_\eta
  \end{pmatrix}.
\]
%
The projection operator onto the column space of $v_\eta$ is $P :=
v_\eta(v_\eta^\top v_\eta)^{-1}v_\eta^\top$, hence the
Schur-complement formula reads
%
\begin{align}\label{eq:si_schur_term}
  \begin{split}
    [\mathbf{J}^{-1}]_{11} &= (v_\theta^{\top}v_\theta - v_\theta^\top v_\eta(v_\eta^\top v_\eta)^{-1}v_\eta^\top v_\theta)^{-1}\\
    %
    %
    %
    &= (v_\theta^{\top} ( I-P ) v_\theta)^{-1}.
  \end{split}
\end{align}
Case~(i) of the main text corresponds to $J_{\theta\theta} =
v_\theta^\top v_\theta$ being small --- $\theta$ barely moves the
trajectory, regardless of $\eta$.

Thus a small CRLB requires $v_\theta^\top(I-P)v_\theta$ large,
i.e.~$v_\theta$ close to orthogonal to $\mathrm{col}(v_\eta)$.
Conversely, if $v_\theta$ lies close to $\mathrm{col}(v_\eta)$, the
CRLB is large.

The implications are immediate. The case when $v_\theta$ can be
approximated well by a vector in the column space of $v_\eta$
corresponds to case~(ii) of the main text: changes in $\theta$ can be
absorbed by changes in the initial conditions, so specifying $\theta$
adds little, and the observed trajectory is reproduced almost equally
well by adjusting the ICs alone.


\section{Data Integration}\label{sec:si_data}

We integrate two sources of US state-level pneumonia-and-influenza
weekly death counts: CDC FluView, covering 2010--2018, and
HealthData.gov, covering 2017--2025. The two sources overlap in
2017--2018. In the overlap period we take, for each (state, week)
pair, the simple arithmetic mean of the two reported death counts;
outside the overlap, the value is taken from whichever source covers
it. The two sources agree closely in the overlap period.

\section{CRLB Ratio}\label{sec:si_crlb_ratio}

The main-text power figure (Fig.~2) operationalizes the CRLB through
a hypothesis-test framework. As a complementary mechanistic view,
Figure~\ref{fig:si_crlb_ratio} shows the ratio of median CRLB
(in-phase / out-of-phase) directly, illustrating how much information
is lost when phase asymmetry is removed.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.75\textwidth]{../pix/crlb_ratio.pdf}
\caption{Ratio of median CRLB (in-phase / out-of-phase) as a function
  of connectivity $\theta$ and seasonal forcing amplitude $\delta$.
  Initial conditions for each simulated season and region are drawn
  independently from the empirical distribution of per-season,
  per-state values fitted to the real data
  (Fig.~\ref{fig:si_ics}). Median ratio is $17.3$; ratio~$>1$ in
  $98\%$ of cells; 95th-percentile ratio is $\sim 850$, maximum
  exceeds $10^{5}$.}
\label{fig:si_crlb_ratio}
\end{figure}
